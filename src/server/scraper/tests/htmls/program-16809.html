Specialization Details

Application Development

Abbreviation: NADE

Length of Study: 2 years

Min. Credits: 120

Degree Programme: Information Technology and Artificial Intelligence

Language of Instruction: Czech

Form of Study: full-time

Accredited from: 2019 Accredited till: 2029

Information for applicants

Key learning outcomes

Students will understand all the layers of the computer based systems including hardware (semiconductor components, logic networks, processors, peripheral devices), software (control and data structures, object orientation, programming languages, compilers, operating systems, databases), as well as their common applications (information systems, computer networks, artificial intelligence, computer graphics and multimedia). They will understand foundations of computer science (discrete mathematics, formal languages and their models, spectral analysis of signals, modelling and simulation). Graduates will be able to analyse, design, implement, test, and maintain common computer applications. They will be able to work efficiently in teams.

Guarantor
Hruška Tomáš, prof. Ing., CSc.
Extent of the State Final Examinations

State Exam in Information Technology and Artificial Intelligence, specialization Application Development consists of the following parts:

  • presentation and defense of master's thesis,
  • oral exam, which combines the basic themes contained in the subjects profiling the basis of Information Technology and Artificial Intelligence (Theoretical Computer Science, Statistics and Probability, Computer Systems Architectures, Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning, Data Storage and Preparation, Functional and Logic Programming, Parallel and distributed algorithms, Modern trends in informatics),
  • oral exam, which combines the basic themes contained in the courses profiling the basis of Application Development (Information Systems Analysis and Design,Distributed Application Environment, Application Development for Mobile Devices, Modern Trends in Informatics, Design of Embedded Systems, User Experience and Design of User Interfaces and Services, Internet Applications).


    All parts of the state examination are held on the same date before the State Examination Board. The state exam can be taken by a student who has obtained the required number of credits in the prescribed composition necessary for the successful completion of the master's degree and submitted the master's thesis in due time. The organization and course of the state examination are given by the corresponding internal standard of the faculty and the relevant instructions of the program guarantor for the state exams.
Examples of theses
  • Instruction set modification for deep neuron net applications
  • Development of big-data processing in the OLTP area
  • Information System for an economic agenda processing
  • Predictive Analysis application in a Industry 4.0. information system
  • Mobile Interface of a student portal

Master's theses are stored at the FIT library, Božetěchova 2, Brno. The list of the master's theses, including the details is available at:
https://www.fit.vut.cz/study/theses/.en
